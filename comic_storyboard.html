Choose-Your-Path Comics

Tap a level. Then tap a choice. See what is right, what is wrong and what comes next.

Start with Level 1 if reading is new. Move up levels when the words feel easy.

Level 1 Very easy words, big pictures

Theme: Sharing a toy One big choice
Start
Sam and Ben see one red car toy.

Sam: I want the car.

Big choice
Sam holds the toy. Ben waits.

What should Sam do?

Right: Sam shares. Ben smiles. They both play.
Wrong: Sam grabs. Ben is sad.
What comes next
If Sam made a wrong choice, he can fix it.

Sam: I am sorry, Ben.

Sam shares the car. Now Ben is happy. They race the car together.

New words in this level:
share, sad, happy, toy, car

Level 2 Easy words, simple sentences

Theme: Waiting your turn One main choice + consequence
Start
Sam and Mia stand in a line for the slide.

Mia: It is my turn now.

Big choice
Sam thinks, “I want to go first.”

What should Sam do?

Right: Sam waits in line. He feels proud and kind.
Wrong: Sam cuts in line. Mia feels mad and hurt.
What comes next
Sam wants to fix his choice.

Sam: I am sorry. You go first.

Mia smiles. She slides. Then Sam takes his turn. Both friends feel good.

New words in this level:
line, turn, proud, mad, fix

Level 3 Medium sentences, moral words

Theme: Telling the truth Two choices, more detail
Start
Sam and his friend Lee play with blocks in class.

Sam: Let’s build a tall tower.

Problem
Sam jumps too close. The tower falls down.

Lee: Oh no! Our tower!

The teacher hears the crash and walks over.

Choice 1
The teacher asks, “What happened to the tower?”

What should Sam say?

Right: Sam says, “I jumped too close and knocked it down.”
Wrong: Sam says, “Lee broke it,” even though it is not true.
What comes next
Truth and fixing the problem.

When Sam tells the truth, the teacher calls him honest and helps them rebuild. Lee feels safe and calm.

New words in this level:
honest, safe, calm, crash, rebuild

Level 4 Longer sentences, more detail

Theme: Keeping others safe Two decisions, clear consequences
Start
At the park, there is a big puddle by the slide.

Sam: I want to run and splash!

Choice 1
A sign says, “No running. Wet ground is slippery.”

What is the safe choice?

Right: Sam walks carefully and keeps everyone safe and dry.
Wrong: Sam runs, slips and bumps into another child.
Choice 2
A younger child sees Sam and starts to copy him.

What should Sam do next?

Right: Sam says, “We must walk. It is slippery,” and shows a safe path.
Wrong: Sam laughs and runs, and the young child almost falls.
What comes next
The grown-up at the park sees what Sam does.

When Sam chooses to walk and help, the grown-up calls him responsible and respectful. Everyone enjoys the park because they feel safe.

New words in this level:
slippery, responsible, respectful, carefully, copy

Level 5 Short paragraphs, strong moral words

Theme: Kind choices all day Three decisions, full story
Morning
Sam walks into class and sees a new student, Ana, standing alone.

Sam remembers how it felt to be new and a little nervous.

Choice 1
The teacher says, “Please make Ana feel welcome.”

What should Sam do first?

Right: Ana feels included and her nervous feeling begins to fade.
Wrong: Ana feels left out and wonders if school will always feel unkind.
Choice 2
Later, during art, there are only two sets of bright markers.

Sam and Ana both reach for the same markers.

Right: Sam suggests a plan, “We can share and trade colors,” which feels fair and friendly.
Wrong: Ana feels ignored, and the teacher must remind Sam about fairness and respect.
Choice 3
At clean-up time, blocks are scattered on the floor.

The teacher asks everyone to help before going outside.

Right: Sam stays to clean, showing he is responsible and helpful.
Wrong: Other students must work harder, and the room stays messy for longer.
What comes next
End of the school day.

When Sam chooses kind and fair actions, Ana feels welcome, the class feels calm, and the teacher trusts Sam to be thoughtful and responsible. These right choices make the whole day better for everyone.

New words in this level:
included, nervous, welcome, fairly, thoughtful, responsible
