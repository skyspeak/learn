Learning to Read – 20 Stories × 5 Levels

Story:

Story 1 – The Red Ball

Theme: pet and play Setting: yard

Level 1: very short sentences, easiest words.

I am Sam. I see a dog. The dog is big. I see a red ball. I get the ball. The dog runs. We play.
Words in this level:
I, am, Sam, see, a, dog, the, is, big, red, ball, get, runs, we, play

Level 2: more detail, a few new words.

I am Sam, and I see a big brown dog in the yard. The dog has a red ball in his mouth. I walk to the dog and pat his head. He drops the ball, and I pick it up. I throw the ball, and the dog runs fast to get it. We play with the red ball again and again.
New words:
brown, yard, has, mouth, walk, pat, head, drops, pick, throw, fast, again

Level 3: full sentences with “and / but / so”.

My name is Sam, and I like to play in the yard after school. A big brown dog runs to me with a bright red ball in his mouth. I want to throw the ball far, but the grass is wet and I might slip. I walk slowly so I stay safe, and the dog waits and wags his tail. When I am ready, I throw the ball high into the air, and the happy dog races after it.
New words:
name, like, after, school, bright, far, might, stay, safe, ready, high, air, races, happy

Level 4: short paragraph, richer description.

Every afternoon, I open the back gate and see the big brown dog waiting for me with his red ball. He prances in the soft grass and wags his tail because he knows we will play our favorite game. I feel excited as I swing my arm and send the ball sailing across the yard. The dog sprints after it, skids in the grass, and then trots back with the ball held proudly in his mouth. We rest for a moment in the shade before we begin another round.
New words:
every, open, back, gate, waiting, prances, favorite, swing, send, sailing, sprints, skids, trots, proudly, shade, begin, round

Level 5: connected short story with time words.

After a long day at school, I hurry home because I know my big brown dog is waiting by the yard gate with his red ball. The air feels cool and a little breezy, and the leaves on the trees rustle softly as we start our game. First I toss the ball gently so the dog can warm up, but soon I throw it higher and farther, and he chases it with endless energy. When the sun begins to slide down and the sky turns orange and pink, we finally slow our running and sit together in the quiet grass. I stroke his warm fur and feel calm and proud, knowing we will play this same happy game again tomorrow.
New words:
long, feels, breezy, leaves, trees, rustle, softly, gently, warm up, farther, endless, begins, slide, finally, stroke, calm, tomorrow

Story 2 – Cat on the Mat

Theme: home and pet Setting: living room

Level 1: very simple pattern.

I see a cat. The cat is on the mat. The mat is red. I pat the cat. The cat purrs.
Words in this level:
I, see, a, cat, the, is, on, mat, red, pat, purrs

Level 2: more actions.

I see my cat on the red mat in the living room. She curls her tail and blinks at me. I sit down and pat her soft fur. She purrs and rolls on her back. I laugh and say, “You like this mat.”
New words:
my, she, curls, tail, blinks, sit, soft, fur, rolls, laugh, like

Level 3: describing the room.

My cat likes to rest on the red mat in the middle of our living room. While I read a book on the couch, she stretches her paws and flicks her tail. If I stop to pat her, she closes her eyes and purrs louder and louder. The sound makes the room feel warm and quiet, like a small, safe nest.
New words:
rest, middle, room, read, couch, stretches, paws, flicks, closes, louder, nest

Level 4: short paragraph with feelings.

Every evening, the red mat in our living room becomes my cat’s favorite spot. She steps onto it, turns in a slow circle, and then curls into a tidy ball. When I sit beside her and gently stroke her back, she purrs like a tiny engine. The light from the lamp makes the mat glow, and the whole room feels calm and cozy.
New words:
evening, becomes, favorite, steps, circle, curls, tidy, gently, stroke, engine, lamp, glow, cozy

Level 5: expanded description, comparison.

On cold days, the red mat in our living room is like a little island of warmth for my cat. She pads over from the window, where the glass feels chilly, and settles on the mat with a long, contented sigh. When I sit nearby to draw or read, she watches my pencil move and then slowly closes her eyes. Her steady purr fills the quiet room, and I feel as peaceful as if we were both wrapped in a soft blanket. Sometimes I think the mat is small, but the feeling it brings is as big as our whole house.
New words:
cold, island, warmth, pads, window, glass, chilly, settles, contented, steady, peaceful, wrapped, blanket, whole

Story 3 – Rainy Day

Theme: weather Setting: home indoors

Level 1: simple weather words.

It is rain. I see the rain. The rain is wet. I sit in the house. I see the rain on the glass.
Words in this level:
it, is, rain, I, see, the, wet, sit, in, house, on, glass

Level 2: activities inside.

Rain taps on the window, and the sky is gray. I cannot play outside, so I build a tall block tower on the rug. Mom gives me a warm drink in a cup. We sit by the window and watch the drops slide down the glass.
New words:
taps, window, gray, cannot, outside, build, tower, rug, warm, drink, cup, drops, slide

Level 3: cause and effect.

When the rain falls hard, the streets outside look shiny and dark. I feel a little disappointed because I wanted to ride my bike, but I also feel cozy inside. Mom and I spread a blanket on the floor and read books together. The soft drum of the rain on the roof makes our house sound like a safe, hidden cave.
New words:
falls, hard, streets, shiny, dark, disappointed, wanted, ride, bike, spread, blanket, drum, roof, hidden, cave

Level 4: longer paragraph, feelings shifting.

At first, I press my nose to the window and sigh because the steady rain ruins my plan to play at the park. But as the day goes on, the room fills with the warm smell of soup, and soft music plays in the background. I draw pictures of umbrellas and puddles while Mom tells me stories about rainy days from when she was little. By the time the clouds begin to break, I feel calm and glad we had a quiet, rainy day together.
New words:
press, nose, steady, ruins, plan, smell, soup, music, background, umbrellas, puddles, clouds, break, together

Level 5: reflection and comparison.

This morning I was sure a rainy day would be boring, because I pictured myself staring out the window and doing nothing at all. Instead, the sound of the rain became a gentle beat that followed us through the day like a song. We cooked soup, read new stories, and even made a tiny paper boat to float in a bowl of water. When the rain finally slowed and a thin strip of blue sky appeared, I realized that staying inside had felt like a cozy adventure. Now, whenever I hear rain on the roof, I wonder what quiet surprises might be waiting for us.
New words:
boring, pictured, staring, gentle, beat, followed, cooked, tiny, boat, float, bowl, finally, strip, realized, adventure, surprises

Story 4 – The Lost Hat

Theme: problem and solution Setting: playground

Level 1: simple loss and find.

I have a hat. It is blue. I go to the park. I run and play. My hat is not on my head. I see my hat on the slide.
Words in this level:
I, have, a, hat, it, is, blue, go, to, park, run, play, not, on, my, head, see, slide

Level 2: more steps in the search.

I wear my blue hat to the playground. I swing, climb, and go down the slide. When I stop to drink water, I see my head is cold. My hat is gone! I look by the swings and under the bench. Then I see my hat hanging on the side of the slide.
New words:
wear, playground, swing, climb, drink, water, cold, gone, look, under, bench, hanging, side

Level 3: emotions and asking for help.

At the park, I run so fast that my blue hat flies off without me noticing. Later, when the wind makes my ears feel cold, I reach up and feel only hair. My stomach twists because I think I have lost my favorite hat. I ask my friend to help me search, and together we walk around the swing set, the sandbox, and the slide. When we finally spot the hat stuck on the rail, we laugh with relief.
New words:
flies, noticing, later, wind, ears, stomach, twists, favorite, search, together, sandbox, rail, relief

Level 4: clear beginning, middle, end.

In the morning, I proudly put on my new blue hat before we walk to the playground. The day is bright and breezy, so I race from the swings to the climbing frame without holding my hat. By the time I sit down for a snack, my hair is messy and my hat has vanished. I feel worried and a little angry at myself, but my friend reminds me that we can look carefully. We retrace our steps and finally find the hat resting on top of the slide ladder, just where the wind left it.
New words:
proudly, new, bright, breezy, frame, snack, vanished, angry, reminds, carefully, retrace, resting, ladder

Level 5: lesson learned.

Losing my blue hat at the playground felt terrible at first, because I imagined it was gone forever. I pictured someone else finding it and wearing it home, while I had to walk back with cold ears and messy hair. But when my friend suggested that we retrace every place we had played, I realized there was still hope. We checked the bench, the swings, the sandbox, and even the bushes by the fence. At last we spotted my hat perched on top of the slide ladder, and I promised myself I would hold it tight on windy days.
New words:
imagined, forever, pictured, suggested, hope, checked, bushes, fence, at last, perched, promised, tight, windy

Story 5 – Picnic in the Park

Theme: family outing Setting: park

Level 1: simple outing.

We go to the park. We sit on the grass. We have a big red bag. We eat and drink. We see a duck.
Words in this level:
we, go, to, park, sit, on, grass, have, big, red, bag, eat, drink, see, duck

Level 2: adding food words.

My family goes to the park with a big red picnic bag. We spread a blue blanket on the soft grass. Mom takes out sandwiches, fruit, and juice. We eat, talk, and watch ducks swim in the pond. The sun feels warm on our faces.
New words:
family, picnic, spread, blanket, soft, takes, out, sandwiches, fruit, juice, talk, swim, pond, faces

Level 3: small problem, small fix.

At the park, we lay our blanket under a tall tree so we can sit in the shade. The wind tries to lift the corners, so we place our shoes on them to keep the blanket flat. We share crunchy carrots and sweet apples while we listen to birds singing. When a curious duck waddles close, we giggle and drop a crumb by accident, then quickly brush the crumbs away.
New words:
lay, tall, shade, corners, place, shoes, crunchy, carrots, sweet, apples, listen, birds, curious, waddles, crumb, accident, brush

Level 4: sequence of events.

First we pick a spot near the pond where we can see the water sparkle in the sun. Next we unfold our blanket and unpack sandwiches, cups, and a cold bottle of lemonade. While we eat, we tell stories and watch clouds drift slowly across the sky. Later, we throw a soft ball back and forth, careful not to toss it into the pond. By the time we pack up, the afternoon light has turned golden, and the park feels calm and gentle.
New words:
first, pick, spot, sparkle, unfold, unpack, cups, lemonade, drift, across, later, toss, pack up, afternoon, golden, gentle

Level 5: reflection on family time.

Our picnic in the park is not fancy, but it feels special because we slow down and enjoy being together. There is no rush to finish eating, so we savor each bite and listen to the sounds around us. Children laugh on the playground, ducks splash in the pond, and the breeze rustles the leaves above our heads. When we finally fold the blanket and head home, my legs are tired from running, but my heart feels light. I decide that simple days like this are my favorite kind of adventure.
New words:
fancy, special, slow down, enjoy, rush, finish, savor, sounds, children, splash, breeze, rustles, finally, decide, simple, adventure

Story 6 – The Busy Street

Theme: safety Setting: city street

Level 1: basic safety idea.

I stand on the path. Cars go on the street. I hold Mom’s hand. We wait for the light. We walk when it is green.
Words in this level:
stand, path, cars, go, street, hold, hand, wait, light, walk, green

Level 2: adding rules.

The street is busy with cars and buses. Mom and I stop at the corner and hold hands. We look left, look right, and look left again. When the little person on the sign turns green, we walk across the street.
New words:
busy, buses, corner, hands, left, right, sign, across

Level 3: clearer steps and reasons.

Our city street is full of cars, bikes, and people in a hurry. Before we cross, Mom squeezes my hand and reminds me to stop at the edge of the sidewalk. We study the traffic, count to three, and wait for the walking signal. When the cars pause and the light changes, we cross together in a straight line.
New words:
city, bikes, hurry, squeezes, reminds, edge, sidewalk, study, traffic, count, signal, pause, straight

Level 4: describing sounds and sights.

The busy street hums with noise as cars roar past, horns beep, and people talk on their phones. All of that sound makes it hard to think, so I am glad Mom holds my hand tightly. We wait at the crosswalk until the red light forces the cars to stop and the walk sign appears. I listen for quiet wheels and look both ways one more time before we step off the curb.
New words:
hums, roar, horns, beep, tightly, crosswalk, forces, appears, wheels, curb

Level 5: small reflection on safety.

Walking along a busy street can feel confusing, because so many things move and make noise at the same time. Cars flash by, bikes weave through gaps, and sirens sometimes scream in the distance. Mom explains that even when we are in a hurry, safety must come first, so we always stop, look, and listen before we cross. As we reach the other side, I feel proud that I remembered each step, and I know that these habits will help keep me safe every day.
New words:
confusing, flash, weave, gaps, sirens, distance, explains, habits, proud, remembered

Story 7 – The Quiet Library

Theme: books and rules Setting: library

Level 1: basic library idea.

We go to the library. Books are on the shelf. We walk, not run. We talk in a soft voice. We pick a book.
Words in this level:
go, library, books, on, shelf, walk, not, run, talk, soft, voice, pick

Level 2: more actions with books.

The library is quiet, so we walk between tall shelves. Mom shows me how to slide a book out gently and hold it with two hands. We sit at a small table and turn the pages slowly. When we are done, we put the book back where it belongs.
New words:
between, tall, shows, slide, gently, hands, table, turn, pages, belongs

Level 3: choosing a book.

I look at rows of colorful book spines and try to choose just one to read. Some books have animals on the cover, and others show rockets, robots, or funny faces. I whisper the titles to myself while I decide. At last I pick a story about a brave cat, and Mom helps me check it out with the librarian.
New words:
rows, spines, choose, cover, rockets, robots, whisper, titles, decide, brave, librarian

Level 4: library as a special place.

The library feels like a quiet forest made of stories, with book shelves standing like tall trees all around us. Light from the high windows falls across the tables where people read and write. When I open my book, the room disappears and the story world fills my mind instead. Even though we hardly speak, I feel close to everyone there, because we are all sharing the same gentle silence.
New words:
forest, stories, standing, around, windows, falls, disappears, world, fills, hardly, sharing, silence

Level 5: reflecting on reading.

Whenever I visit the library, I feel like I am choosing a new door to open in my mind. Each book holds a different place, a different time, or a different voice that wants to speak to me. The quiet rules in the library are not just about being polite; they help every reader travel in their own imagination. When I leave with a book tucked under my arm, I carry more than paper and pages—I carry the promise of another adventure waiting for me at home.
New words:
whenever, choosing, different, polite, travel, imagination, tucked, promise, adventure

Story 8 – The Big Birthday

Theme: celebration Setting: home party

Level 1: simple party.

It is my big day. I see a cake. I see a hat. My friends clap. I blow out one candle.
Words in this level:
it, is, my, big, day, see, cake, hat, friends, clap, blow, out, one, candle

Level 2: more birthday details.

Balloons hang from the wall, and a bright banner says “Happy Birthday.” My friends wear silly hats and hold small gifts. We sing together while Mom brings in the cake. I make a wish and blow out all the candles at once.
New words:
balloons, hang, banner, happy, birthday, silly, gifts, sing, wish, once

Level 3: games and sharing.

At my birthday party, we play simple games like “Pin the Tail” and “Pass the Parcel.” Everyone laughs when the blindfold slips and someone pins the tail on the wall. After the games, we share slices of cake and pass around paper cups of juice. I feel proud and a little shy when my friends shout my name.
New words:
party, simple, games, pin, tail, parcel, blindfold, slices, around, proud, shy

Level 4: focusing on feelings.

When I wake up on my birthday, my stomach feels full of tiny, fluttering butterflies. All day I wait for the moment when my friends will ring the doorbell and rush inside with gifts and smiles. During the party, I feel excited, noisy, and a little overwhelmed by all the voices and music. Later, when the guests leave, the house is quiet again, and I slowly open each card and remember every kind word.
New words:
wake, stomach, fluttering, butterflies, moment, ring, doorbell, rush, inside, excited, overwhelmed, guests, card, remember, kind

Level 5: looking back on the day.

At the end of my big birthday, I sit on my bed and think about every part of the day. The colorful balloons are drooping, the cake plates are stacked in the sink, and bits of wrapping paper still sparkle on the floor. I feel tired but happy, because my friends and family used their time to make this day special for me. As I fall asleep, I realize that the best gift was not the toys or treats, but the feeling of being loved and remembered.
New words:
end, sit, bed, drooping, stacked, sink, wrapping, sparkle, tired, treats, loved, remembered

Story 9 – Night at Home

Theme: bedtime routine Setting: home at night

Level 1: simple bedtime.

It is night. I put on my red bed shirt. I brush my teeth. I get in bed. Mom turns off the light.
Words in this level:
night, put, on, bed, shirt, brush, teeth, get, in, mom, turns, off, light

Level 2: adding order of steps.

First I clean up my toys and put them in the box. Then I brush my teeth until they feel smooth. I change into my soft pajamas and climb into bed. Mom tucks the blanket under my chin and kisses my head goodnight.
New words:
first, clean, toys, box, then, until, smooth, change, pajamas, climb, tucks, blanket, chin, kisses, goodnight

Level 3: adding sounds and small worries.

When the house gets quiet at night, I hear new sounds. The fridge hums, the pipes tick, and a car moves slowly down the street. Sometimes these noises make me feel a little nervous. Mom reminds me that the sounds mean our house is working and we are safe inside.
New words:
fridge, hums, pipes, tick, slowly, noises, nervous, reminds, working, safe

Level 4: using imagination.

The shadows on my wall look like mountains and trees when the night-light glows. I imagine tiny explorers climbing the peaks and camping under glowing stars. As Mom reads a story in a soft, sleepy voice, my eyes grow heavy. Little by little, the pictures from the book mix with my own dreams.
New words:
shadows, wall, mountains, trees, night-light, imagine, explorers, peaks, camping, glowing, stars, sleepy, heavy, mix, dreams

Level 5: comfort and reflection.

Lying in my bed at night, I think about everything that happened during the day. Some moments were noisy and busy, but now the room is still and calm. I listen to the slow rhythm of my breathing and feel the steady hug of the blanket around me. Soon, my thoughts drift away like clouds, and I fall asleep feeling safe, warm, and ready for tomorrow.
New words:
lying, happened, moments, still, rhythm, breathing, steady, hug, drift, clouds, asleep

Story 10 – The Little Seed

Theme: growth Setting: garden

Level 1: planting a seed.

I have a seed. I dig a hole. I put in the seed. I pat the dirt. I give it a drink.
Words in this level:
have, seed, dig, hole, put, in, pat, dirt, give, drink

Level 2: waiting for growth.

I plant a little seed in a pot by the window. Each morning I give it a small cup of water. I wait and wait, but I only see brown dirt. One day a tiny green tip pushes up to say hello.
New words:
plant, little, pot, window, morning, cup, water, tiny, tip, pushes, hello

Level 3: noticing changes.

Every week the seedling changes a little more. First it grows two small leaves, then four, and then many. I turn the pot so each side can feel the sun. The stem becomes stronger, and the leaves reach toward the light.
New words:
week, seedling, changes, grows, leaves, turn, side, feel, stem, stronger, reach

Level 4: moving outside.

When the plant is too big for the pot, we move it into the garden. I dig a wider hole and tuck the roots carefully into the cool soil. Rain falls and soaks the ground, and sun warms the leaves. Soon the plant stands taller than my knees, and tiny buds begin to appear.
New words:
move, garden, wider, tuck, roots, carefully, soil, falls, soaks, taller, knees, buds, appear

Level 5: connecting to patience.

Watching the little seed grow into a strong plant takes many days, and sometimes I feel impatient. On cloudy mornings it hardly seems to change at all, but beneath the soil the roots keep spreading. Mom tells me that plants teach us to be patient, because they move slowly but never stop. When bright flowers finally open, I remember every time I watered, waited, and believed the seed would grow.
New words:
watching, strong, many, impatient, cloudy, beneath, spreading, teach, slowly, never, finally, watered, believed

Story 11 – Snowy Hill

Theme: seasons Setting: winter hill

Level 1: snow and sled.

Snow is on the hill. I sit on my sled. Dad holds the rope. We go down fast. We laugh in the snow.
Words in this level:
snow, on, hill, sit, sled, dad, holds, rope, go, down, fast, laugh

Level 2: getting ready for cold.

We bundle up in coats, hats, and warm gloves. The hill behind our house is covered in white snow. I sit in front on the sled while Dad pulls us to the top. The air feels cold on my cheeks, but I am excited to ride.
New words:
bundle, coats, hats, warm, gloves, behind, covered, white, pulls, top, cheeks, excited

Level 3: describing the ride.

From the top of the hill, the world looks small and bright. Dad counts “one, two, three,” and then gives the sled a push. We slide over bumps and leave a long, wavy line in the snow. The wind rushes past my ears, and I shout with joy all the way down.
New words:
top, world, small, bright, counts, push, slide, bumps, wavy, line, wind, rushes, joy

Level 4: more reflection on winter.

We climb the snowy hill again and again until our legs feel heavy. Each time the sled races down, it carves a deeper path in the snow. My nose tingles and my fingers feel stiff, but I do not want to stop. Winter days like this make the cold air feel friendly instead of sharp.
New words:
climb, again, legs, heavy, races, carves, deeper, path, tingles, stiff, instead, sharp

Level 5: ending warm inside.

When we finally head home from the snowy hill, my boots squeak and crunch with every step. Inside, we peel off our wet coats and hang them near the heater. As I wrap my hands around a warm mug of cocoa, I watch snow still falling outside the window. The memory of flying down the hill stays bright in my mind, even as my cheeks slowly lose their chill.
New words:
finally, head, boots, squeak, crunch, peel, heater, mug, cocoa, memory, flying, lose, chill

Story 12 – The New Bike

I see my new bike. It is red. Dad holds the seat. I push the pedals. The bike goes!

afraid, without, moment, scared, rolling, feet, end, block, realize

Learning to ride my bike means I tip over sometimes. My knees get small scrapes, and my eyes fill with tears, but I stand up again. Dad reminds me that every rider wobbles before they glide. With each try, my turns get smoother and my legs feel strong.

Level 5: feeling of freedom.

When I finally ride my bike all the way around the block, the wind brushes my face and I feel light as a bird. The houses slide past, the wheels hum under my feet, and my fear has turned into joy. Story 13 – At the Farm

Theme: animals Setting: farm

Level 1: naming animals.

I go to a farm. I see a cow. I see a pig. I see a hen. I see a big red barn.
Words in this level:
go, farm, see, cow, pig, hen, big, red, barn

Level 2: simple sounds.

The cow says “moo,” and the pig says “oink.” The hens cluck and peck at the ground. I stand by the fence and watch them eat. The farmer waves and lets me pat the gentle cow.

Level 3: jobs on the farm.

The farmer shows me how to feed the chickens and fill their water tray. We collect warm eggs from the nests and carry them in a basket. Later, we toss hay to the cows and listen to them chew. I learn that taking care of animals is busy work every single day.

shows, feed, chickens, fill, tray, collect, nests, basket, toss, hay, chew, taking care, single

Level 4: describing the farm.

The farm smells like hay, mud, and fresh grass all mixed together. Tractors rest near the barn, and tall rows of corn sway in the wind. Chickens scratch for seeds while cats nap in the sun. Everywhere I look, something is growing, clucking, or buzzing.

smells, fresh, tractors, rest, rows, corn, sway, scratch, seeds, nap, everywhere, growing, clucking, buzzing

Level 5: connecting food to farm.

Before I visited the farm, eggs and milk just came from the store, and I did not think much about them. Now I know hens lay the eggs we eat for breakfast, and cows give the milk we pour on our cereal. The farmer works early in the morning and late at night to feed, clean, and protect the animals. When I drink a glass of milk at home, I remember the farm and feel thankful for all that quiet, careful work.
New words:
visited, breakfast, cereal, early, late, protect, remember, thankful, careful

Story 14 – The Lost Tooth

Theme: growing up Setting: home and school

Level 1: wiggly tooth.

My tooth is loose. I wiggle it. I bite a red apple. My tooth comes out! I see a small gap.
Words in this level:
tooth, loose, wiggle, bite, apple, comes, out, see, small, gap

Level 2: telling others.

All day at school, I push my loose tooth with my tongue. At snack time I crunch into an apple, and the tooth falls into my hand. My friends shout, “Let us see!” The teacher gives me a little envelope to keep the tooth safe.
New words:
day, school, push, tongue, snack, crunch, falls, hand, shout, envelope, safe

Level 3: mixed feelings.

When I look in the mirror, the empty space in my smile seems strange. I feel proud because losing a tooth means I am growing, but I also feel a little nervous about the new tooth coming in. Mom says my grown-up teeth will be strong and bright. I touch the gap with my tongue again and practice my new smile.
New words:
mirror, empty, space, strange, proud, losing, growing, nervous, grown-up, strong, bright, practice

Level 4: night-time tradition.

That night I slip the tiny tooth into a small box and place it under my pillow. I wonder if I will hear any sounds in the dark room. The pillow feels lumpy, but I keep it there anyway. Soon I drift off to sleep, dreaming about shiny coins and new teeth.
New words:
slip, tiny, box, place, pillow, wonder, sounds, dark, lumpy, anyway, drift, dreaming, coins

Level 5: connecting to growing up.

In the morning, the box under my pillow holds a small coin instead of my tooth. I smile at the trade and realize that my body is quietly changing all the time. New teeth, taller legs, and stronger hands all mean I am growing older. Losing a tooth felt scary at first, but now it feels like a little step toward being big.
New words:
morning, holds, coin, trade, realize, quietly, changing, taller, stronger, older, step

Story 15 – The Tall Tree

Theme: nature Setting: yard / park

Level 1: very simple tree.

I see a tree. It is tall. Birds sit on it. The tree gives shade. I sit under the tree.

Level 4: tide and time.

As the tide comes in, the water climbs higher on the sand. Our sandcastle slowly melts away, even though we try to fix the walls. Mom says the sea is always moving, pushing and pulling the shore. We watch the waves erase our footprints and draw new lines in the sand.

Level 5: remembering the day.

On the drive home from the beach, sand still clings to my toes and my hair smells like salt and wind. I feel sleepy from running, digging, and jumping over waves.
